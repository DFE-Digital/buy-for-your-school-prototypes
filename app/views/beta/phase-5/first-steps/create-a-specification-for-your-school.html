4. Create a specification for your school

Use the Create a specification service to build a specification that automatically includes mandatory standards and tells suppliers what you need them to know.

{% from "govuk/components/accordion/macro.njk" import govukAccordion %} {{ govukAccordion({ id: "accordion-default", items: [ { heading: { text: "What is a specification used for?" }, content: { html: "

A specification is a document that explains to potential suppliers the goods or services that your school needs.

Depending on the procurement process you follow, you will share your specification at some point before choosing your preferred supplier. Suppliers may use this to decide:

  • whether to bid for a contract
  • what to include and focus on in their proposal
  • the scale of the goods or services you need
  • how well suited they are to working with your school
" } }, { heading: { text: "Consider what suppliers need to know" }, content: { html: "

Your specification should include:

  • a precise description of what you need
  • an explanation of what it should do, if needed
  • the amount you need
  • what quality it should be
  • when you need it
" } }, { heading: { text: "Think about the long term" }, content: { html: "

Think about what is essential, what would be nice to have and what you will need in 1 or 2 years time.

Check you have included everything you need. If you miss something out it may cost more once you have a supplier, as there will no longer be other people competing to supply it cheaper.

If you cannot work out all the things you need to know for your specification there may still be other ways to buy, but you should get expert advice.
